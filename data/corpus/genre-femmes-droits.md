# Genre, Femmes et Protection des Droits en Afrique

## 1. Autonomisation économique femmes

### Participation main-d'œuvre
- Taux emploi: 45-55% femmes (vs 75-85% hommes)
- Secteurs: Agriculture 50%, commerce petite-échelle 35%, salarial 15%
- Salaires: 30-40% moins hommes même travail
- Sous-emploi: 25-35% femmes part-time/casual work

### Obstacles entrepreneuriat femmes
- Capital accès: 15-20% femmes obtiennent crédit formel
- Collateral: 85% femmes sans guarantee foncière
- Formation: 40% femmes entrepreneur manquent skills
- Temps disponible: Travaux ménage 5-8h/jour reduce production

### Succès modèles women business
- Groupes femmes: 5-50 personnes, capital pooling tontines
- Secteurs viables: Transformation aliments, textiles, services
- Fintech: Mobile money accès improved 3x par 5 ans
- Microcrédit: 10-15% clients femmes, taux remboursement 95%+

### Revenus impacts
- Income femmes: Household spending education +40% vs men control
- Autonomy: Revenue own = decision-making power increase
- Investissement enfants: Femmes priorité education daughters
- Épargne: Women groups accumule capital +200% 3 years

## 2. Violence based on gender

### Prévalence troubling
- Violences intimes: 30-50% femmes lifetime physical/sexual
- Enfants mariage: 30-40% filles <18 ans zones rurales
- Harcèlement sexuel: 60-80% femmes public spaces report
- Féminicide: 3-5 morts 100k femmes rates zones conflict

### Impacts santé reproductive
- Grossesses forcées: Issues health complications maternal
- IST transmissions: Violence associated+60% infections
- Avortements: 40% forced terminations post-violence episodes
- Trauma psych: PTSD, depression 40-60% abuse survivors

### Justice system gaps
- Poursuites: 5-10% violence cases reach police
- Convictions: 1-3% cases convicted (faible de reports)
- Délais: 2-5 years justice proceedings exhausting
- Impunité: Societal normalization, weak enforcement

### Interventions probantes
- Crisis centers: Hotlines 500-2,000 calls/month croissance
- Legal aid: Gratuit services increased access 10x
- Shelters: Limited 20-50 places networks, high demand
- Community education: Men engagement 40% reduction violence zones cibles

## 3. Santé reproductive + maternelle

### Planification familiale uptake
- Méthodes modernes: 20-30% women using contraception
- Injectables: Marché prédominant 35-40% utilisateurs
- PIU/stérilet: <5% uptake despite effectiveness
- Connaissances gaps: 40-50% women ignorent options

### Accouchements assistance
- Assistance qualifiée: 60% urban vs 35% rural births
- Complications: 15-20% births need intervention
- Décès maternels: 300-600 per 100k live births Africa
- Post-partum care: 30-40% women no follow-up 6 weeks

### Allaitement support
- Initiation: 80-90% first 1 hour (encouragement amélioration)
- Durée exclusive: 40-50% 6 months (sous-objectif 50%+)
- Support groups: 20% zones accessibilité, impact +5%
- Employment: Travail impacte allaitement, policies manquent

### Santé mentale périnatal
- Dépression postpartum: 10-15% women affected
- Psychosis: <1% incidence rare serious
- Support limited: <5% women reçoivent mental health care
- Integration health services: Screening 30% facilities seulement

## 4. Droits fonciers femmes

### Propriété disparité
- Ownership: 10-20% femmes seules terre title
- Access: 50% femmes access par family members
- Succession: Veuves excluded 70% cas property post-mort

### Réformes légales
- Lois égalité: 35+ countries have updated inheritance laws
- Implementation gaps: Application 30-50% seulement
- Awareness: 40% femmes ignorent droits légaux
- Contestation: Cultural resistance slows progress

### Collective land models
- Women groups: Acquisition collective safer
- Productivity: Collective farms +30% yields
- Retention: Security improves post-acquisition

## 5. Accès services services basiques

### Éducation
- Completion primary: 75-80% boys vs 60-65% filles
- Secondary access: 45% boys vs 30% girls enrollment
- Obstacles: Cost, distance, domestic responsibilities, marriage

### Santé
- Maternity care: 70% women had some prenatal care
- Infections: STI testing <20% women access
- Contraception counselling: 30-40% facilities provide

### Justice
- Access courts: Limited especially rural areas
- Customary law: 50-70% justice through traditional leaders
- Gender-sensitive: Very few courts ensure equity
